Copyright Policy

Complaints procedures

The University believes in resolving any complaints as quickly as possible and as close to the source of the problem as possible.

Should you have a complaint about any information used from the journals published, please put your complaint in writing to the relevant officer as follows:

Copyright concerns, School of Computing, Newcastle University: journal-of-computing-copyright@newcastle.ac.uk

Copyright

All content published on the server is a copyright © of Newcastle University except for any materials that has been explicitly marked as being the copyright of another person. All rights reserved.

Individuals are allowed to download or copy materials on this website for their own personal use only. Proposals for reproduction should be made to: journal-of-computing-copyright@newcastle.ac.uk

Notice and Takedown Period

Contact details

The University seeks to observe any compliance with the law and rights of rights holders while publishing journal articles to support both student and staff contributions.

If you are a rights holder and concerned that your material has been found on your website or legitimately under our name elsewhere without your permission, or is not covered by a limitation or exception in the laws of the UK, please contact us in writing to the following email: journal-of-computing-copyright@newcastle.ac.uk

Please include the following information in the email:

  • Your contact details
  • Full bibliographic details of the materials
  • Exact URL or other location you have found the material
  • Proof that you are a rights holder and a statement, under penalty of perjury, you are the rights holder or an authorised representative.

Procedure

Upon receiving the the notification, a receipt will be sent and the “Notice and Takedown Procedure” will be invoked.

  1. An acknowledgment receipt of your enquiry will be sent by email or letter within 10 working days. An initial assessment will be able on the validity and plausibility of the complaint.
  2. If the initial assessment is potentially valid, further investigation or legal advice will be made. The relevant material will be removed while pending for an outcome of the complaint.
  3. The author and supervisors will be notified and contacted of the complaint and will be encouraged to assuage the complaints concerned.
  4. Both the complainant and author and supervisors will be encouraged to resolve the issue as quickly and amicably and to the satisfaction of both parties. Some possible outcomes include:
    • Material is replaced in the journal article is unchanged
    • Material is replaced in the journal article with changes
    • Material is permanently removed from the journal article
  5. If no agreement can be reached, the material will remain unavailable until a resolution has been made.
  6. Notice will be given via website to inform others of the takedown and others who may have copies until a resolution has been reached.
  7. We will take legal advice and support against unofficial sites purporting to be in our name/ not under our control that breach others’ rights
  8. Special additional takedown procedures that may apply in special circumstances
