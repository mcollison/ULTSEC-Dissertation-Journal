Peer Review Guidelines

This is a guideline for students to make their peer review submission.

  1. Each student will be given 4 journal articles to review
  2. ALL students are required to read through the entire journal article at least once
  3. Each student will be given a section to review, which can be either one of the followings:
    • Introduction
    • Methodology and Background
    • Findings and Discussion
    • Conclusion
  4. Students are required to provide feedback for the section they are given to review as follows:
    Reviewer's Decision Accept, Minor Revisions, Major Revisions
    Understanding of research area In a range of 1 to 5, 1 being a novice and 5 being an expert, please state how well you understand the research area.
    Confidence in Review In a range of 1 to 5, 1 being the least confident and 5 being the most confident, please state how confident you are in this review.
    Summary A brief summary of the article and its contribution to the research domain. Have a first glance on the article and evaluate if it is publishable or not. Make an initial assessment based on the question, approaches and conclusions. The first two paragraphs will summarise the research question addressed and its contribution. Are the objectives/ goals clearly addressed? Evaluate on the methods used to address the problem and if the data generated support the conclusions. If there is a fatal flaw in the paper that cannot be fixed, in your opinion, please explain why with evidence to support it.
    Merits Include the positive aspects of the paper and provide reasons on why you think the paper has merits. For example, it could be solving a problem which is hard to solve or has yet to have a solution. Or perhaps, using a method that is new.
    Major Revisions Include any flaws you think there may be in the paper that needs revising and provide a reason why with concrete ways to address the problem. For example, you may want to provide an alternative method and why that will be a better approach with citations from literature.
    Minor Revisions Minor errors in the paper like grammatical or spelling mistakes, mislabelled figures or tables. Evaluate on the flow of the paper and if all references, data and background research are present. You are not required to catch every spelling or grammatical mistakes but the scientific assessment of the paper.
  5. Students are required to submit their reviews into the journal system.
    • Student logs into system and and under the article to review, click on “Accept Review, Continue to Step #2”
    • Under tab 3, “Download & Review”, student reviewer can download the journal article and type their review based on on the guidelines provided in the text box as shown:
    • A recommendation is required as either accept, minor revisions or major revisions. In this case, we will not reject articles. At the end of it, please click on “Submit Review”.
